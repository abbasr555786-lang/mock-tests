JMI PhD Exam Pattern

Two-part written test — Paper-I is an objective MCQ paper of general aptitude, reasoning, current affairs and subject awareness; Paper-II / interview is research-method and discipline specific · Medium: English (the question paper for the Humanities & Languages faculty is set in English)

SectionQuestionsMarks
Paper-I — Objective MCQs (general knowledge, current affairs, reasoning, language & literature, research awareness)100100
Total — 120 minutes100100

Marking scheme: +1 for each correct answer; there is NO negative marking in Paper-I (100 MCQs, 1 mark each).

Pattern is taken from the official instruction page of the digitised paper (Paper Code H-01, Faculty of Humanities & Languages, PhD Entrance 2021-22). Paper-I carries 100 one-mark MCQs to be done in 2 hours with no negative marking; weightage between the written test and the interview varies by department as per UGC and JMI rules. The JamiaPrep mock reproduces the objective Paper-I. Always confirm the current pattern and the eligibility/weightage with the official JMI PhD admission notification for your chosen faculty.

JMI PhD Syllabus — Section by Section

General Knowledge & Current Affairs

  • National & international current affairs
  • Government schemes & flagship programmes
  • Science, technology, space & defence
  • Awards, books & authors
  • Sports & important days

History, Polity & Society

  • Modern Indian history & the national movement
  • Indian Constitution & polity
  • Social reform movements
  • Jamia Millia Islamia history
  • Sociology & psychology fundamentals

Language & Literature

  • English grammar, idioms & comprehension
  • Indian English & Hindi literature
  • Urdu & Persian classics
  • Authors, works & literary awards

Reasoning & Quantitative Aptitude

  • Coding-decoding & letter series
  • Alphabetical & dictionary ordering
  • Number series & arithmetic puzzles
  • Analogies & logical sequences

Research & General Science Awareness

  • Basic physics, chemistry & biology
  • Geography & resources
  • History of science & mathematics
  • Ancient Indian knowledge texts

Eligibility for JMI PhD Entrance Exam

  • A Master's degree (or a recognised equivalent) in the relevant discipline, normally with at least 55% marks (relaxation for reserved categories as per JMI/UGC rules).
  • Candidates qualified in UGC-NET/JRF, GATE or an equivalent national fellowship may be eligible for exemption from the written test as per the notification.
  • Refer to the official Jamia Millia Islamia PhD admission notification for the exact qualifying-degree, percentage and category criteria for the given cycle.

How to Apply

Mode: Online only — there is no offline/paper form.

Application fee: ₹700 per programme (₹1,000 for some self-financed programmes) — non-refundable, paid online. Confirm the exact amount for your course on the portal.

  1. Register on admission.jmi.ac.in with your name, a working email and mobile number.
  2. Fill the application form — personal details, qualifying-exam marks, and select PhD (Doctor of Philosophy) research programmes as your programme.
  3. Upload a scanned photograph and signature in the prescribed format and size.
  4. Pay the application fee online (UPI, debit/credit card or net banking).
  5. Download and save the confirmation page — you will need it at the time of admission.
